Notes of GFH1000: The Symposium


Vocabulary
treatise, [C]n.

He (Socates) was interested in moral issues and philosophized neither by giving lectures nor by writing treatises, but by talking with other people.

interlocutor, [C]n.

Socrates examined the views of his interlocutors by means of questions and answers to look into the logical coherence of the proposed views.

dialectic

Since dialogue is the way by which Socrates practiced philosophy his method is known as dialectic.
